The Legend of Zelda is a high fantasy action-adventure video game franchise created by the Japanese game designers Shigeru Miyamoto and Takashi Tezuka. It is primarily developed and published by Nintendo. The series revolves around different incarnations of the main character named Link, a young woman named Zelda, and the primary antagonist named Ganon.

Here is the time line of all The Legend of Zelda games:

  • August 22, 1987 - The Legend of Zelda was released on Famicon and eventually the Nintendo Entertainment System, otherwise known as the NES. The game introduce a new level of gaming that included roleplaying, action, adventure, and puzzle/logic.
  • December 1, 1988 - The Adventure of Link was released originally released on the Famicon in Japan and released on the NES in North America. The game switched to a side-scrolling field of play rather than the top down prespective for which the series became known for.
  • April 13, 1992 - A Link to the Past was the first and only game to be released on the Super Nintendo Entertainment System. This game returned to the top down prespective and introduced the concept of two different worlds, one dark and one light. The game was rereleased on the Game Boy Advance in 2002.
  • August 6, 1993 - Link's Awakening was the first title to be released on Handheld on the Game Boy. A color updated version (Link's Awakening DX) was released for the Game Boy Color. The game was also remade for the Nintendo Switch and was released on September 20, 2019.
  • November 23, 1998 - Ocarina of Time was released on the Nintendo 64 and is listed as one the greatest games ever created by numerous websites and magazines. This game was the first in the series that was visually displayed in 3D. This game was remade for the Nintendo 3DS and was released in June 2011.
  • October 26, 2000 - Majora's Mask was released on the Nintendo 64 similar to its predecessor. The game revolved around a time system in which the player must reset the cycle every three days. This game was released as a sequel to Ocarina of Time and was also remade for the Nintendo 3DS, released in February 2015.
  • May 14, 2001 - Oracle of Seasons released alongside Oracle of Ages on the Game Boy Color, this game and its partner can be linked together to form a single, linear plot with an alternative ending. A remade version was released on the Nintendo 3DS on February 27, 2013.
  • May 14, 2001 - Oracle of Ages released on the Game Boy Color, was also remade on the Nintendo 3DS and released on the same date as its partner.
  • December 2, 2002 - Four Swords was released alongside the remade version of A Link to the Past on the Game Boy Advance. It was the first multiplayer The Legend of Zelda game to be released. As part of the 25th anniversary of The Legend of Zelda the game was released on the Nintendo DSi and the 3DS for a limited time. It is the first of the Four Swords series.
  • March 24, 2003 - The Wind Waker was the first game to be released on the Nintendo GameCube and was the first game in the series to employ cel-shading, resulting the game to have a cartoon-like appearance. The game was remaster for the Wii U console and was released in the fall of 2013.
  • June 7, 2004 - Four Swords Adventures was the first game to incorporate multiplayer elements into its main campaign. The game was released on the GameCube and allowed the player to control multiple Links.
  • January 10, 2005 - The Minish Cap was released on the GameBoy Advance and featured a fully explorable land of Hyrule.
  • November 19, 2006 - Twilight Princess was released for both the GameCube and the Wii. This installment was the first Zelda game to be rated T by ESRB. The game was remastered and released on the Wii U in March 2016.
  • October 1, 2007 - Phantom Hourglass was the first Zelda game to be released on the Nintendo DS and is a direct sequel to The Wind Waker.
  • December 7, 2009 - Spirit Tracks was the second Zelda game to be released on the Nintendo DS with a limited edition bundle only being released in Europe.
  • November 20, 2011 - Skyward Sword was the first Zelda game to be created specifically with the Wii in mind and to require Wii Motion Plus. A HD remaster of the game was released on July 16, 2021 for the Nintendo Switch.
  • November 22, 2013 - A Link Between Worlds was the frist Zelda installment to be developed for the Nintendo 3DS in mind and is an indirect sequel to A Link to the Past.
  • October 23, 2015 - Tri Force Heroes was released on the Nintendo 3DS and had multiplayer with Link being accopmained by two other Links.
  • March 3, 2017 - Breath of the Wild was released on both the Wii U and the Nintendo Switch. The game featured a vast open world for Link to explore. Unlike the other Zelda games, Breath of the Wild does not enforce a specific order in which the quests or dungeons need to be solved in.
  • 2022 - Sequel to Breath of the Wild is an upcoming main installment in the The Legend of Zelda series and is scheduled to be released in 2022.
